Sylvia Targ for ASUC Senate

Sylvia's Platforms

Sylvia Targ is running to represent the environmental community in the ASUC Senate. If elected, she will work tirelessly toward the following goals and more.

Institutionalizing Sustainability

  • Organize and support zero waste coalition to assess Zero Waste by 2020’s failure and the path forward
  • Carbon-free by 2050: creating ambitious and feasible campus plan and timeline
  • Examining unsustainable campus partnerships (e.g. Pepsi)

Accessibility and Centralization of Environmental Resources

  • Create pathways with mentors to link first years and transfers to environmental work
  • Maintain a frequently updated website with information on environmental organizations
  • Environmental class catalog with reviews
  • Campus Environmental Summit once a semester for the community to celebrate our work and organize across groups for the next term

Increasing Intersectionality in Environmentalism

  • Improving environmental curriculum and increase inclusion of social issues in environmental courses
  • Facilitate student involvement in community based environmental justice work by partnering with and uplifting local grassroots organizations

Sylvia's Qualifications

Not only does Sylvia have bold ideas, she's also working towards implementing them now.

Sustainability

Sylvia has worked for housing & dining sustainability since she was a first year. She currently serves as a food literacy coordinator for the campus and has worked on many projects from zero waste to food security to addressing labor standards in the food system to reducing CO2 emissions.

Environmental Resources

Sylvia is entrenched and up to date on happenings in the environmental community, and there's nothing she loves more than connecting the community with resources to help them get involved. If elected, she will be able to do this on a much more impactful level.

Intersectionality in Environmentalism

Sylvia has been guest lecturing in courses for 2.5 years on the intersection of environmental science & justice. Last semester she was a UGSI for an environmental course and worked to improve the curriculum to address environmental justice. She has also presented her work on the intersection of oceans and environmental justice internationally. She has direct action organizing experience and has been proud to be a member of the Students of Color Environmental Collective (SCEC) since her freshman year.
